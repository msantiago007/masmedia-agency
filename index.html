My world, my work

Marco Alejandro Santiago is the founder of MAS media and a media and technology professional with over 20 years of experience designing and delivering digital systems across creative, nonprofit, and enterprise environments. As a software and IT project manager at the Writers Guild of America West, he works closely with developers, business stakeholders, and end users to deliver platforms that support the organization's mission, governance requirements, and long-term operational integrity, including an AI-driven document management system with machine learning categorization and natural language search. His work emphasizes clear requirements, accountable delivery, and systems that stay understandable and maintainable over time.

He is also the founder of Stackwright, a governance-first applied AI platform that generates production-ready full-stack applications from requirements documents. Stackwright pairs a multi-agent generation pipeline with a self-learning knowledge graph and built-in controls for compliance, security, and auditability, exploring how AI-assisted systems can be made inspectable, secure, and continuously improving while keeping human judgment firmly in the loop. The approach is informed by an applied mathematics and engineering background in systems analysis and probabilistic reasoning, treating AI and machine learning not as opaque tools but as decision-making systems whose assumptions, failure modes, and feedback loops must be explicitly understood and designed for.

His core competencies span business systems analysis, project and program management, process and business re-engineering, and the design of complex, human-centered digital workflows, built over engagements with Red Bull, American Express, Oracle, Paramount, Sony Pictures, and WorldatWork. In parallel he has built a career in film and media: as CEO of EXcel Endeavor Media and former VP of Creative Development at SunCast Entertainment, he produced the theatrically released feature WATER & POWER, developed at the Sundance Labs and executive produced by Edward James Olmos. He holds the PMI PMP and Google Project Management certifications, completed advanced coursework in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning through Stanford University Online, and is currently in Stanford Graduate School of Business's Launching a Startup program. He studied Applied Mathematics and History at Arizona State University and completed UCLA's Professional Producers and Professional Writing programs.
